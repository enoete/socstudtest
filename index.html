Amazing Earth Facts!

Earth is one of the eight major planets that follow their own paths around the sun. The sun is just one of hundreds of millions of stars in our galaxy! Because the sun is so large, its gravity causes the planets to constantly orbit around it.

Life on Earth could not exist without heat and light from the sun! Without the sun, Earth would be a cold, dark, lifeless rock floating in space.

The sun is 93 million miles away from Earth, but its light reaches us in just 8 minutes and 20 seconds!

üåê The Four Physical Systems

Hover over each system to see it grow!

1. ATMOSPHERE üå§Ô∏è

The layer of air around the earth. It protects us from the sun's harmful rays and provides the oxygen we breathe.

2. HYDROSPHERE üíß

All the water on Earth: oceans, rivers, lakes, groundwater, and ice. About 70% of Earth's surface!

3. LITHOSPHERE üèîÔ∏è

Earth's crust including land (mountains, valleys, deserts) and underwater land (ocean floors). About 30% of Earth's surface.

4. BIOSPHERE ü¶ã

All living things - plants, animals, and humans. Supported by the other three systems!

Important Note:

The earth is tilted 23.4 degrees on its axis (we say 23.5 for accuracy).

All four systems are connected! Plants (biosphere) need water (hydrosphere), soil (lithosphere), and air (atmosphere) to grow.

üíß The Water Cycle

What is the Water Cycle?

The water cycle is the continual movement of water around the earth.

The Water Cycle Steps

1. EVAPORATION ‚òÄÔ∏èüíß

The sun heats up water, it turns into water vapor and rises into the air.

2. CONDENSATION ‚òÅÔ∏è

Water vapor cools down and turns back into little droplets, forming clouds.

3. PRECIPITATION üåßÔ∏è‚ùÑÔ∏è

When clouds get too heavy, rain, snow, or hail falls to Earth.

4. COLLECTION üåä

Water collects in rivers, lakes, and oceans.

The water you drink today could be the same water that dinosaurs drank millions of years ago!
About 97% of Earth's water is saltwater in the oceans. Only 3% is freshwater!

üåä Bodies of Water

Two Kinds of Water

Freshwater: Found in rivers, lakes, and groundwater

Saltwater: Makes up most of Earth's water and is found in the oceans and seas

üèûÔ∏è Rivers - Nature's Highways

Rivers: Flowing bodies of freshwater that move from high areas to lower areas, often emptying into oceans.

Examples: Nile, Amazon, Mississippi, Yangtze

Importance: Provide water for drinking, irrigation, and transportation

üìç Lakes

Lakes: Large inland bodies of still freshwater or saltwater

Examples: Lake Victoria, Great Lakes, Caspian Sea (largest lake)

Importance: Habitat for fish, water for cities, and recreation

üíß Groundwater

Groundwater: Water found beneath Earth's surface in soil and rock layers

Importance: Supplies wells and springs, especially in dry regions

üåä Oceans

Oceans: Largest bodies of saltwater covering most of Earth's surface

The Five Major Oceans: Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic

Importance: Regulate Earth's climate, support marine life, provide food and transport routes

üåü Why Are Water Bodies Important?

  • Support Life: Water supports life for humans, animals, and plants
  • Multiple Uses: Used for drinking, irrigation, travel, recreation, and electricity generation
  • Shape Earth: Help shape landforms and influence climate

The Continental Shelf: The part of the shallow ocean water near continents

Ocean Floor: Has mountains, valleys, and plains just like land!

If all the world's water fit in a gallon jug, the freshwater available for us to use would equal only about one tablespoon!
The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is a mountain range under the ocean that's longer than the Andes, Rockies, and Himalayas combined!

Landforms are the different shapes and features you see on the earth's surface. They are created by nature and come in many sizes and types.

üèûÔ∏è VALLEYS

Famous Landforms

Mount Everest: The tallest mountain in the world at 29,032 feet (8,849 meters) above sea level. Located in the Himalayas, on the border between Nepal and Tibet.

The Grand Canyon: One of the most famous natural landmarks, carved by the Colorado River in Arizona, USA. It stretches approximately 277 miles (446 kilometers) long and plunges over one mile deep!

The Grand Canyon is so big you could fit about 19 Statue of Liberties stacked from bottom to top!

üó∫Ô∏è Plate Movements

What are Tectonic Plates?

Earth's crust is made up of 16 large plates called tectonic plates. They move only a few inches each year, but over millions of years, they can move thousands of miles.

Types of Plate Movements

1. üîÑ CONVERGENT BOUNDARIES (plates move toward each other)

  • Mountains: When two plates collide
  • Volcanoes: When an oceanic plate slides under another plate
  • Deep Ocean Trenches: Where one plate goes under another

2. ‚ÜîÔ∏è DIVERGENT BOUNDARIES (plates move away)

  • Rift Valleys: On land
  • Mid-Atlantic Ridge: In the ocean
  • New Crust or Islands: From magma rising and cooling

3. ‚ö° TRANSFORM BOUNDARIES (plates slide past each other)

  • Faults: Cracks in Earth's crust
  • Earthquakes: When plates slide past each other

Weathering and Erosion

üåßÔ∏è WEATHERING

Breaking down rocks into smaller sediments by wind, ice, rain, and plants

üèîÔ∏è EROSION

Natural forces like wind, water, and ice moving weathered materials to new places, slowly shaping landforms

The Himalayan Mountains (including Mount Everest) were formed when two continental plates collided about 50 million years ago!
It took the Colorado River about 6 million years to carve the Grand Canyon through erosion!

üë• Human Impact on Water

üö® How Humans Have Damaged Water

  • Pollution from factories and farms has polluted rivers, lakes, and oceans
  • Groundwater pollution when chemicals seep into the soil, harming plants, animals, and people
  • Acid rain when chemicals in the air mix with water, damaging trees, fish, and soil

‚úÖ What We Can Do to Help

  • Conserve water - turn off taps, take shorter showers
  • Don't pollute - dispose of trash properly
  • Support clean water initiatives in your community
  • Learn about and protect local waterways
  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle to prevent pollution
Every person can make a difference! Small actions like turning off the tap while brushing your teeth can save gallons of water every day.
